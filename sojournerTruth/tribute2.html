Sojourner Truth was born into slavery. In 1826 Truth escaped slavery and helped lead other slaves to freedom using the Underground Railroad. She is most known for her "Ain't I a Woman?"speech delivered amid a lifetime of activism, at the Ohio Women's Rights Convention in Akron, Ohio in May of 1851. Amongst other activists Truth made a stand to fight for equality in the United States of America. She has many other speeches about abolotionism and equal rights for all. Truth pursued political equality for all women and spoke against other abolitionists for not pursuing civil rights for all black men and women. For instance, her many contributions to the women's suffrage movement. She helped recruit black troops for the Union Army during the civil war.
